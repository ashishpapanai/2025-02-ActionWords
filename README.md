> TLDR; The dataset is collected as a part of the International Social Survey Programme (ISSP) 2022. ISSP is is a continuous programme of cross-national collaboration running annual surveys on topics important for the social sciences. 
The data can be accessed here: [link](https://search.gesis.org/research_data/ZA10000)

## Dataset Summary: Family, Work, and Gender Roles Survey
The data covers five main areas: attitudes, family labor, social support, finances, and detailed demographics.

### 1. Attitudes and Gender Roles

* **Mothers' Employment:** Attitudes on working mothers' effect on children and family life; views on whether a job or home/children is the "real want" for women; fulfillment of being a housewife.
* **Suitability and Responsibility:** Opinions on men's/women's responsibility for the home, how well mothers/fathers are suited for childcare, and who is best suited for **top leadership positions**.
* **Employment Extent:** Preferred work level for women when children are **preschool age** and **school age**.

### 2. Family Structure and Childcare

* **Attitudes on Family:** Views on children affecting freedom/social standing, necessity of marriage for having children, and acceptability of **cohabitation**.
* **Alternative Forms:** Attitudes toward **single-parenting** and childcare by **same-sex couples**.
* **Parental Tasks:** Opinions on which parent *should* handle specific duties for a **five-year-old** (e.g., financial provision, daily care, discipline, role model).
* **Parental Leave:** Attitude toward paid leave, preferred **duration (in months)**, and **division** between parents.
* **Childcare/Elderly Support:** Who should primarily **provide** and **cover the costs** for preschool childcare and help for the elderly.

### 3. Time, Labor, and Work-Family Balance

* **Time Budget:** **Hours per week** spent by **both partners** on household work and caring for family members.
* **Duty Allocation:** Who performs specific chores (**cleaning, cooking, laundry, shopping, care for the sick**).
* **Fairness:** Estimation of **fair share** of household work between partners.
* **Conflict:** Frequency of difficulties due to **work interfering with family** and **family/household duties interfering with work**.
* **Income Organization:** How income is organized in the marriage/partnership.


### 4. Social Networks and Finances

* **Kinship Responsibility:** Opinions on responsibilities toward relatives in specific need situations (e.g., lodging, financial aid).
* **Social Preference:** Reliance on and importance of **close relatives vs. close friends**.
* **Financial Assessment:** **Household's ability to make ends meet** and comparison of current financial situation to 12 months prior.
* **Happiness:** General feeling of **happiness in life**.

### 5. Demographics and Employment Details

* **Respondent Demographics:** Sex, age, education (ISCED), religion, voting/political stance, ethnic group, household size/composition, migration background, and income (personal/household).
* **Employment Details:** **Hours worked weekly**, occupation (ISCO), supervisory role, and current status for **both the respondent and partner**.
* **Upbringing (Optional):** Which parent was more involved in the respondent's upbringing and their children's upbringing.
